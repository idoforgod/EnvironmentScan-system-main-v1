### 8.4 FSSF Classification Methodology

The Future Signal Scanning Framework (FSSF) 8-Type Classification system used in this report follows the futures studies standard framework:

**Priority CRITICAL types:**
- **Weak Signal**: Early indicators of potential change; limited evidence, high ambiguity; require careful monitoring for pattern confirmation. Characterized by: low visibility, low certainty, potentially high impact.
- **Wild Card**: Low-probability, high-impact events that could rapidly transform the operating environment. Characterized by: surprise factor, severe consequences, limited preparation time.
- **Discontinuity**: Breaks in previously stable trends or systems that signal regime change. Characterized by: deviation from established patterns, systemic stress indicators.

**Priority HIGH types:**
- **Driver**: Active forces causing change in the environment. Characterized by: clear causal mechanism, measurable impact, policy or market relevance.
- **Emerging Issue**: Issues gaining visibility and traction but not yet institutionalized or widely recognized. Characterized by: growing mention frequency, stakeholder mobilization, policy attention.
- **Precursor Event**: Specific events that signal larger transformations ahead. Characterized by: first-of-kind occurrence, precedent-setting nature, systemic implications.

**Priority MEDIUM types:**
- **Trend**: Well-established directional movements with accumulated evidence. Characterized by: multi-year data support, broad recognition, gradual rather than sudden.
- **Megatrend**: Long-duration, large-scale transformation processes spanning decades. Characterized by: global scope, structural nature, irreversibility within planning horizons.
