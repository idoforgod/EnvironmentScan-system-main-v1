### 8.4 FSSF Classification Methodology

The Future Signal Scanning Framework (FSSF) classifies signals into 8 types based on their maturity, impact scope, and uncertainty level:

- **Weak Signal**: Early, ambiguous indicator of potential change. Low visibility, high uncertainty. Requires sustained monitoring to determine if it strengthens into an emerging issue.
- **Wild Card**: Low-probability, high-impact event that materializes suddenly. Today's Khamenei assassination and Operation Shield of Judah are textbook Wild Cards that have materialized.
- **Discontinuity**: Abrupt break from established patterns. Iran's retaliation against 5 Gulf states is a discontinuity -- it breaks the expected pattern of bilateral retaliation.
- **Driver**: Active force shaping outcomes in a specific direction. China's military purge and EU Fit for 90 are drivers with clear directional impact.
- **Emerging Issue**: Rapidly developing situation with escalation potential. Taiwan arms delay and China drone tactics are emerging issues that could escalate or stabilize.
- **Precursor Event**: Early event that may foreshadow larger systemic change. Supreme Court expansion is a precursor to potential shifts in Korean corporate law.
- **Trend**: Established pattern with momentum. TSMC revenue growth, Korea exports, and AI adoption are confirmed trends.
- **Megatrend**: Deep structural force operating over decades. The semiconductor $1T trajectory and multi-front Islamic world crisis represent megatrend-level forces.
