### 8.4 FSSF Classification Methodology

The Future Signal Scanning Framework (FSSF) classifies signals into 8 types based on their probability, impact, and temporal characteristics. WF4 applies this framework across multilingual global news sources, enabling cross-cultural signal detection:

- **Wild Card**: Very low probability events that have materialized with very high impact. The Iran war is the textbook example.
- **Discontinuity**: Breaks in established patterns indicating structural change. Gulf aviation disruption (1,400+ flights) represents a Discontinuity.
- **Precursor Event**: Events signaling potential larger changes. Russia-China alignment and Pezeshkian's transitional government are Precursor Events.
- **Driver**: Forces actively shaping change trajectories. EU energy policy and China's semiconductor plan are Drivers.
- **Emerging Issue**: Issues gaining visibility and momentum. China's deflation risk and EU electricity paradox are Emerging Issues.
- **Trend**: Established directional movements. Japan defense spending and Asia AI outlook are Trends.
- **Weak Signal/Megatrend**: Not represented in this crisis-dominated scan period.
